# Lead Hunter Scoring Rubric

This rubric scores Australian businesses for fit with Hourglass, a Melbourne AI consultancy that builds custom AI agents for operational workflows. A strong lead should have public evidence of operational complexity, a real workflow pain, and a plausible path to a focused first agent build.

Positive criteria:
- Australia/Melbourne relevance: 0-15. Prioritise Australian companies, Melbourne presence, Australian customers, or explicit local operations.
- Clear operational workflow pain: 0-20. Look for named workflows such as support triage, reporting, onboarding, compliance, logistics coordination, finance ops, or document handling.
- Evidence of scale or growth: 0-15. Give credit for hiring, multi-site operations, national reach, funding, expansion, or large customer/team signals.
- Evidence of manual/repetitive work: 0-15. Reward public mentions of queues, spreadsheets, reporting, approvals, exception handling, invoice follow-up, or repeatable coordination.
- Fit for Hourglass-style AI agents: 0-20. Score higher when a narrow custom agent could prepare work, route tasks, extract documents, draft summaries, or keep humans in approval loops.
- Reachability / clear next step: 0-5. Prefer companies with a clear website, public context, and a sensible review or outreach path.
- Evidence quality: 0-10. Company-owned sources and specific job/news evidence score highest.

Negative adjustments:
- Not Australia-relevant: -20
- No clear business pain: -20
- Too small, hobby, or irrelevant: -15
- No usable evidence: -25
- Likely AI automation consultancy competitor: -20
- Blocked, private, or unverifiable source: -25

Tier thresholds:
- A: 80-100, strong lead.
- B: 70-79, good lead.
- C: 60-69, maybe, not final CSV unless configured.
- Reject: below 60 or unsupported/unsafe.
